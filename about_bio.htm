BIOGRAPHICAL NOTES

Born in Munich, Germany, Dora arrived in India in 1983 with the burning desire in her heart to learn Kathak. Soon she became the disciple of internationally renowned dancer and choreographer Padmashri KUMUDINI LAKHIA at "Kadamb" School of Dance and Music in Ahmedabad, Gujarat and was awarded an ICCR scholarship.
After a period of many years of preservance and commitment to her Gurus training, Dora passed her exams with distinction and emerged as a solo dancer, greatly appreciated for her technical virtuosity, her stilistic purity and her sensitive expressiveness. She was member of the 'Kadamb performing group'.
Holding a postgraduate degree in educational science from Munich University, Dora did her M.A. in Indian philosphy at the GujaratUniversity in Ahmedabad.

1990 dora returned to Germany, after six months of final training at the renowned "Shri Ram Bharatia Kala Kendra" in New Delhi under SHRI RAM MOHAN MISRA, the son of Kumudiniben's legendary GURU SHAMBHU MAHARAJ. She taught the ballet group there her Guru's style and choreography. Dora gave numerous solo performances in Delhi, Bhopal, Bangalore, Karachi, London, Zurich, Italy and many other places.

TANHAI (lonelyness)

The culmination of her career in India have been performances for the Prime Ministers Rajiv Ghandi and V.P. Singh.

Living halfyearly in New Delhi and Munich since 1997, Dora enriches her repetoire (abhinaya, bhava) since then under the loving guidance of GURUJEE MUNNA SHUKLA at the Kathak Kendra, the country's leading Kathak school.

She toured Germany with modern interpretations of Urdu poetry, performing "TANHAI", choreographed by Kumudini Lakhia. It is a poem written by Mohammed Iqbal, highlighting a womans search for answers to her feeling of solitude.

She performed as SATJAVAT and YAMA in the dance drama "SAVITRI", which was directed and choreographed by her in cooperation with Sandra Bannerjee and greatly acclaimed by the audience.
