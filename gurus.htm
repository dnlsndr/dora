Padmashri Kumudini Lakhia

 
SHRIMATI KUMUDINI LAKHIA

was chela (disciple) of the legendary GURU SHAMBHU MAHARAJ. She has taken up the challenge of extending and enlarging the classical repertoire to include subjects relevant to a contemporary context and revolutionised the traditional world of KATHAK with her unique development of modern group- and solo choreographies. For her, the classics will always be a source of inspiration and instruction, however, experiment and innovation are essential to the survival of a tradition.
Kumudini feels the necessity to enrich and refine traditional arts with present day energy, to demystify indian dance forms for the benefit of a deeper understanding of their content.
Her innovative creations are artistic experiments, utilizing the rich potential of classical Indian dance principles to explore spacial and dynamical possibilities and to express themes with social relevance in a contemporary context. Her dance centre 'KADAMB' in Ahmedabad embodies todays spirit of dance in India and has, with its discovery of new dimensions, found worldwide recognition and admiration.

SHRI MUNNA SHUKLA

A rare combination of technical dexterity and creativity, Shri Munna Shukla or 'Munnaji' as he is familiarly known, is the grandson of the Kathak maestro Shri Sunder Lal Shukla. Shri Munna Lal Shukla joined the Bharatiya Kala Kendra in 1961 as a staff artist, working in close association with SHRI BIRJU MAHARAJ, his maternal uncle, who later accepted him as his discipline under the National Scholarship Scheme.
From 1968–75 he imparted training to advanced students of Kathak at the Kela Chhaya, Pune.
He has choreographed many dance numbers and several full lenght dance-dramas. Shri Mannu Lal Shukla has given numerous successful solo performances and is a teacher of considerable merit.
Since 1976 he has been teaching Kathak at the Kathak Kendra, New Delhi, as Sahachari Pradhyapak. He is a recipient of the Uttar Pradesh Sangeet Natak Akademie Award.
