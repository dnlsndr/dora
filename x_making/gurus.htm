Padmashri Kumudini Lakhia

 
Kumudini Lakhia

was chela (disciple) of the legendary Guru Shambhu Maharaj. She has taken up the challenge of extending and enlarging the classical repertoire to include subjects relevant to a contemporary context and revolutionised the traditional word of Kathak with unique development of modern group- and solo choreographies. For her, the classics will always be a source of inspiration and instruction, however, experiment and innovation are essential to the survival of a tradition.
Kumudini feels the necessity to enrich and refinde traditional arts with present day energy, to demystify indian dance forms for the benefit of a deeper understanding of their content.
Her innovative creations are artistic experiments, utilizing the rich potantial of classical indian dance pronciples to explore spacial and dynamical possibilities and to express themes with social relevance in a cpntamporary context. Her dance centre 'Kadamb' in Ahmedabad embodies todays spirit of dance in India and has, with its discovery of new dimensions, found worldwide recognition and admiration.

Munna Shukla

A rare combination of technical dexterity and creativity, Shri Munna Shukla or 'Munnaji' as he is familiarly known, is the grandson of the Kathak maestro Shri Sunder Lal Shukla. Shri Mannu Lal Shukla joined the Bratiya Kala Kendra in 1961 as a staff artist working in close association with Shri Birju Maharaj, his maternal uncle, who later accepted him as his discipline under the National Scholarship Scheme.
From 1968-75 he imparted training to advanced students of Kathak at the Kela Chhaya, Pune.
He has choreographed many dance numbers and several full lenght dance-dramas. Shri Mannu Lal Shukla has given noumerous successfull solo performances and is a teacher of considerable merit.
